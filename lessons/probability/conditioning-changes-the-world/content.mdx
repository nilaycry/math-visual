conditional probability is often taught as a formula you plug into after the fact.

the better picture is harsher and cleaner: once you condition, you are not in the old world anymore.

---

### the formula

if `P(B) > 0`, then

<div className="math-highlight">P(A | B) = P(A ∩ B) / P(B)</div>

students usually memorize the division and move on. what the denominator is doing is the important part.

conditioning on `B` means only outcomes inside `B` are still visible. the old sample space gets cut down, and then the remaining probabilities are rescaled so they add up to `1` again.

---

### the picture

the numerator says how much of the old world belonged to both `A` and `B`.

the denominator says how much world is left after restricting to `B`.

</div>

once that restriction happens, the proportions inside `B` matter more than the absolute areas from the original picture.

this is why conditioning often feels unintuitive at first. it changes the reference frame.

---

### independence is the case where nothing changes

events `A` and `B` are independent when learning one does not change the probability of the other:

<div className="math-highlight">P(A | B) = P(A)</div>

equivalently,

<div className="math-highlight">P(A ∩ B) = P(A)P(B)</div>

that equation is not the definition you should start with. the better starting sentence is: knowledge of `B` leaves the probability of `A` untouched.

the product rule is what that sentence turns into algebraically.

---

### where bayes lives

bayes' rule is just the same relationship solved in the other direction:

<div className="math-highlight">P(A | B) = P(B | A)P(A) / P(B)</div>

it is useful because it tells you how to reverse the conditioning when one direction is easier to model than the other.

but the spirit is unchanged. conditioning still means the world has been cut down and renormalized.

---

### what this does not cover yet

this lesson is keeping everything event-based. conditional expectation is a deeper version of the same idea, and it deserves its own treatment later.

for now, the main shift is enough: conditioning is not a decorative extra label. it changes the space you are living in.
